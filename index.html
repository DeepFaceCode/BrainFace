Research question

Identity beyond appearance

Face categories look remarkably similar. BrainFace asks whether subtle identity information is nevertheless measurable in short, non-invasive EEG recordings.

Q1

Is identity decodable?

Can face-evoked EEG distinguish 40 individual identities substantially above the 2.5% chance level?

Q2

Is it stable over time?

Do learned identity representations survive changes across independently recorded sessions?

Q3

Is it shared across people?

To what extent do identity-related neural patterns transfer to previously unseen participants?

Central finding

Facial identity is reliably encoded in EEG signals, but its representation is shaped by temporal context, session variability, and familiarity.

03

EEG-FOFNO

Local topology meets global spectrum

Two complementary branches operate on the same EEG tokens before adaptive fusion predicts one of 40 identities.

Branch A

Electrode Topology Encoding

Temporal convolutions extract short-range waveform patterns while electrode-wise aggregation captures coordinated neural activity across channels. Transformer blocks then model long-range dependencies among temporal EEG patches.

  • Local spatiotemporal tokenization
  • Multi-head self-attention
  • Context across processing stages
Branch B

Weighted Fourier Spectral Encoding

The full spectrum is retained rather than hard-truncated. A learnable decay function emphasizes stable low-frequency structure while softly regulating noisy high-frequency variation.

  • Full-spectrum Fourier transform
  • Learnable frequency rebalancing
  • Global neural dynamics
Fusion

Adaptive identity prediction

Temporal-contextual and frequency-domain representations are aligned and fused, then flattened into a lightweight classifier for 40-way identity prediction.

Z = ZT + ZF

05

Beyond average accuracy

What shapes neural identity?

The benchmark exposes how presentation history, recording time, participant variability, and familiarity affect identity-level decoding.

Temporal context

Continuity helps—but can confound.

Longer identity continuity substantially improves recognition, yet extreme blocks may amplify contextual and block-related effects. BrainFace uses ICL-50 as a practical balance.

Cross-session stability

Identity persists across recording days.

Under leave-one-session-out evaluation, average performance remains above chance at 5.0% Top-1 and 22.5% Top-5, despite electrode, attention, and state variability.

Chance 2.5% 5.0%

Cognitive factors

Familiarity matters more than gender.

Recognition rates are nearly identical across male and female identities, while highly familiar identities tend to appear in the upper half of the accuracy ranking.
